# Chapter 5 — Time Value of Money and Project Valuation

The **time value of money** is the principle that a dollar today is worth more than a dollar
in the future, because a dollar today can be invested to earn a return.

**Discounting** converts a future cash flow into its value today. The present value of a cash
flow `FV` received in `n` years at discount rate `r` is `PV = FV / (1 + r)^n`.

**Net present value (NPV)** sums the present values of all of a project's cash flows,
including the initial outlay. Decision rule: accept a project when its NPV is positive.

The **internal rate of return (IRR)** is the discount rate that makes a project's NPV equal
to zero. NPV is generally preferred to the payback period, because payback ignores both the
time value of money and any cash flows beyond the cutoff date.
